SkyShear shows where the forecast says turbulence is likely — it cannot see or measure the actual air.
  It presents model-forecast turbulence potential (NOAA GFS, ~13–25 km grid, hourly)
  interpolated along your pointing direction — it does not and cannot measure the air in view.
  Values are computed from real forecast physics — vertical wind shear and Richardson-number stability
  (the standard clear-air-turbulence diagnostics), plus the Ellrod index in high-detail mode — but the
  EDR-style number is an uncalibrated estimate, not a measured EDR. Not an aviation weather product;
  never use for flight decisions.
