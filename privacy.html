SkyShear Privacy Policy

Effective July 17, 2026

SkyShear is a turbulence-forecast viewer. It has no accounts, no sign-in, no advertising, no analytics, and no tracking of any kind.

What the app uses, and where it goes

  • Location (Point-at-sky page) — used on your device to work out which patch of atmosphere you are pointing at. Derived coordinates (latitude/longitude of sky sample points) are sent to the free Open-Meteo weather API to retrieve the forecast. No identifier, account, or device information accompanies those requests, and SkyShear does not store your location anywhere.
  • Motion & orientation sensors — processed entirely on your device to compute where the phone is pointing. Never transmitted.
  • Camera (optional) — if you enable the AR background, the camera image is shown on screen only. It is never recorded, stored, or transmitted.
  • Flight search (Flight forecast page) — the origin and destination airports you pick and the departure time are sent to Open-Meteo (as route coordinates) to fetch the forecast. If you optionally add an AviationStack API key, the airport pair is also sent to AviationStack to list real flights. The Flight forecast page does not use your location, motion sensors, or camera.

What we store

On your device only: the app caches its program files so it opens quickly, and — if you use the optional flight list — stores your AviationStack API key and a one-day cache of flight results in your browser's local storage. None of this leaves your device except as described above, and it is removed when you clear site data or uninstall the app. SkyShear has no servers and receives nothing.

Third parties

External services contacted for data: Open-Meteo (their terms) — always; AviationStack (their terms) — only if you add your own key, which receives that key and the searched route; airline logo images from the avs.io logo service (receives only the airline's two-letter code); and Google Fonts for typefaces. None receives any personal information from SkyShear beyond the technical minimum of an HTTP request.
